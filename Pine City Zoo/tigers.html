TIGER

The tiger (Panthera tigris) is the largest species among the Felidae and classified in the genus Panthera. It is most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange-brown fur with a lighter underside. It is a carnivore and primarily preys on ungulates such as deer and wild boar. The tiger is the national animal of India, Bangladesh, Malaysia and South Korea.

tigers are apex predators, primarily preying on ungulates such as deer and wild boar. They are territorial and generally solitary but social animals. They communicate through vocalisations, visual signals and scent markings.

The tiger is the largest extant cat species, most recognisable for its dark vertical stripes on orange-brown fur with a lighter underside. It is a carnivore and primarily preys on ungulates such as deer and wild boar.

The tiger is the national animal of India, Bangladesh, Malaysia and South Korea. It is also the state animal of the Indian states of Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Punjab and Uttarakhand. The tiger is listed in CITES Appendix I and is endangered on the IUCN Red List.
